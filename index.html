What's a hackathon?

A hackathon is an opportunity for creative thinkers of all kinds to come together to solve problems, make connections, and learn. All who are interested in tech and willing to learn are welcome!

About CodeGate

CodeGate is a 12 hour hackathon hosted by the <Colgate Coders> and sponsored by Major League Hacking. This is Colgate University’s first campus Hackathon! There will be various workshops throughout the day, as well as the chance to design, build, and present your own project in teams of 3-4 people. As any other Major League Hacking sponsored event, we follow the MLH Code of Conduct. By registering to our event, you are committed to ensuring a safe and friendly environment for all people at the event!

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can attend CodeGate?

Anyone who is interested in learning more about technology or creating a tech project!

Do I need to know how to code?

Nope! There will be workshops at the event. You may also choose to work with a group and contribute your ideas.

Do I need to stay the full 12 hours?

Absolutely not! Although breakfast, lunch, and dinner will be provided, participants are free to come and go as they please. There will be various snacks and beverages provided, as well as a break room with games for those who decide to stay.

What should I bring?

A laptop (and charger) and a willingness to learn! You may also want to bring planning materials such as a notebook and pencils.

Do I need to have a team?

No, there will be a chance to form teams at the hackathon. Teams may have up to four members. You may also choose to work on your own.

Will there be prizes for submitted projects?

Of course! There will be a 1st, 2nd, and 3rd place prize, as well as a <Colgate Coders> prize and a Best Newbie Hack prize.

Do you provide travel reimbursements?

We will not be providing reimbursements this year.

I don’t see my college on the registration form. What do I do?

Just select the "Other" option and register! Looking forward to seeing you at the event.
